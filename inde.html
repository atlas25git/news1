HERE’S WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW It’s still not over. Assuming Joe Biden wins Arizona, which the Associated Press has called but others haven’t, a victory in any of the currently undecided battleground states—with a narrow Biden lead in Georgia and lawsuits flying around—will put him in the White House. Meanwhile, Donald Trump repeated baseless allegations of fraud. The markets appear to be pricing in a Biden victory. Stocks in prison companies are way down, with the Democrat promising to change the US’s policies on immigration and asylum, “end prolonged detention, and reinvest in a case management program.” Meanwhile, the Chinese yuan is at its strongest versus the US dollar in more than 10 years. Cable news dominated election night TV ratings, thanks to older viewers. While regular broadcast TV suffered a drop-off from 2016, cable offers popular anchors and expensive graphics that attract more concerned—and partisan—viewers, and also has advantages over the internet. Social media companies are failing to stop election misinformation in Spanish. While they appear to have done a decent job moderating content in English, a language in which their policies and processes are more robust, Spanish-language Facebook and Twitter accounts with large followings continue to make false claims.
